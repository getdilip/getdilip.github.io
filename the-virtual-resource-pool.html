The Virtual Resource pool

Dilip Singh 24 Nov 2017 on Life-experience

I have seen few HR people on LinkedIn discussing the poor joining ratios and asking why some candidates don’t pick up their calls after the interview schedules.

I have a similar question to them from a candidate’s point of view, why do some companies hire candidates and never send them the offer letter?

Two years back I had to travel ~30 km for the Accenture interview. It was a mega drive being conducted from multiple locations for people with 2 to 11 months of experience. There were two rounds: Amcat based aptitude test followed by the technical + HR (both elimination). I was able to clear them all.

A few days later they confirmed my onboarding over a mail and asked to fill up the EAF form. After another few days, I was asked to upload my academic and other documents. Their portal started showing my status as pending for offer letter.

I waited for few weeks and when there were no further updates I wrote to them asking for the same. They replied me saying that there was no open demand matching my profile at that time and requested me to wait for some more days.

This whole process took 3-4 months but there was no response yet. I wrote to them again and asked whether they will send me the offer or not? They replied that they have archived my profile as per their policy and I need to reapply again. Slow Claps!

These same events happened to my another friend who attended this from a different location and many others whom I also know.

However, wasting our time must not have been their aim. So, why did they do this?

Was this done to get some new project showing a “virtual resource pool”, this is what I concluded.
